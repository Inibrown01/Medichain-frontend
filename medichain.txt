1. Executive Summary
Medichain NG is a web-based platform designed to combat the alarming proliferation of counterfeit drugs and substandard medical equipment in Nigeria. The platform leverages blockchain technology — specifically an EVM-compatible chain with IPFS-based document storage — to create an immutable, transparent, and publicly queryable registry of verified pharmaceutical products and medical devices.

This document outlines the complete scope of work, technical architecture, budget breakdown, payment schedule, and project timeline for the development of a fully mobile-responsive web platform with public-facing verification, manufacturer portals, and an admin dashboard.

3. Proposed Solution: Medichain NG
3.1 How It Works
Medichain NG creates a tamper-proof, blockchain-anchored registry of approved pharmaceutical products and medical equipment in Nigeria. Each verified product receives a unique on-chain identifier that consumers, pharmacists, healthcare workers, and regulators can query in real time.

Step	Actor	Action
1	Manufacturer / Importer	Submits product documentation, batch records, and certificates
2	Admin / Regulator	Reviews submission, approves or rejects via dashboard
3	Smart Contract	Mints a unique product record on the EVM blockchain; documents stored on IPFS
4	QR Code / Serial	Generated and provided to the manufacturer for product labeling
5	Consumer / Pharmacist	Scans QR code or enters serial number on the web platform to verify authenticity
6	Platform	Returns real-time verification result: GENUINE / FLAGGED / NOT REGISTERED

3.2 Blockchain & Storage Architecture
•EVM-Compatible Chain: Polygon (recommended for low gas fees) or BNB Smart Chain
•Smart Contracts: Written in Solidity — product registry, ownership, batch verification
•IPFS Storage: Product documents, certificates, and images stored on IPFS via Pinata or Web3.Storage
•On-chain: Product ID, NAFDAC registration number, manufacturer hash, status (approved/recalled)
•Off-chain (IPFS): Certificates, lab reports, product images, full metadata — linked by CID hash
•Wallet integration: MetaMask / WalletConnect for admin signing; users interact without a wallet

3.3 Platform Features
Public-Facing Features
•Homepage with product search bar (by name, batch number, NAFDAC reg. number, or QR scan)
•Real-time verification result page: product details, manufacturer, approval status, recall alerts
•Mobile-responsive design — works on all screen sizes including low-end Android phones
•Report suspicious product form — submit reports with photo upload
•Publicly viewable product registry with pagination and filters
•NAFDAC recall alerts and public notices section

Manufacturer / Importer Portal
•Secure login — register as a manufacturer or authorized distributor
•Submit new products for verification (form + document upload to IPFS)
•Track submission status (Pending / Under Review / Approved / Rejected)
•View all registered products and batch records
•Download QR codes and verification labels for approved products

Admin Dashboard
•Secure admin login with role-based access (Super Admin, Reviewer, Read-only)
•Review and approve/reject product submissions with notes
•Issue product recalls — instantly updates on-chain status
•Manage manufacturers and distributors (KYC, license verification)
•Analytics dashboard: total products verified, search queries, reports filed
•Export reports as CSV / PDF
•System settings: manage categories, product types, regulatory standards
•Audit trail — all admin actions logged immutably on-chain

9. Why Blockchain for Drug Verification?
Traditional centralized databases are vulnerable to manipulation, data corruption, and single points of failure. Using an EVM blockchain provides:

•Immutability: Once a product record is written on-chain, it cannot be altered or deleted — preventing fraud
•Transparency: Anyone can independently verify a product's registration status without trusting a central authority
•Decentralization: The registry does not depend on any single server; it lives on the blockchain network
•Auditability: Every approval, recall, and status change is recorded with a timestamp and signer address
•Interoperability: Other applications, labs, or regulators can read the registry via standard smart contract calls
•IPFS for documents: Documents are content-addressed — the hash proves the document hasn't been tampered with

This makes Medichain NG fundamentally more trustworthy than existing NAFDAC web portals, which are centralized and prone to downtime, data gaps, and manipulation.
